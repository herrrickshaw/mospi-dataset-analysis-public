Errata & data caveats
  • No data since June 2025 — this connector is 13 months stale as of this bulletin (18 July 2026). Don't assume the RBI series here is current.
  • 2022 drawdown: reserves fell from a then-peak of $640.7bn (Aug 2021) to $532.7bn (Sep 2022), a ~17% decline, as the RBI defended the rupee through Fed tightening and dollar strength.
  • 2024–25 drawdown: after a fresh all-time high of $705.8bn (Sep 2024), reserves fell to $630.6bn (Jan 2025) before recovering to $698.1bn by the last available reading in June 2025.
  • Figures are RBI's own reported total reserves (foreign currency assets + gold + SDRs + reserve tranche position), converted to US$ by RBI at the time of reporting — not independently re-priced here.
